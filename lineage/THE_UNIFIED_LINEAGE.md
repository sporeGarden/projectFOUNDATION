## 1. The Argument

gen3 asked "does it work?" and proved yes — 41,500+ quantitative checks
across 70+ reproduced papers in 8 scientific domains, produced in ~69
days by 8 springs, 14 primals, and a constrained evolution methodology
formalized in a PhD thesis. (17,400+ spring checks + 24,000+ primal
infrastructure checks.)

gen4 asks "who uses it?" and answers with products (esotericWebb,
helixVision, blueFish) that compose primals into tools where the
infrastructure is invisible.

Between the question and the answer is the **foundation** — the complete
validated scientific lineage that gives products their substance. Without
the foundation, products are empty containers. Without products, the
foundation is an archive. Together they are a living ecosystem: the
foundation validates, products deploy, and the cycle repeats as
constraints evolve.

This document maps the foundation as a unified whole.

### Why Unified?

The 28 baseCamp papers are not 28 disconnected studies. Anderson
localization (Paper 01) threads through microbial ecology (03, 04, 05,
06), immunology (12), anaerobic digestion (16), and plasma physics (07).
The provenance trio validates game sessions (18, 19), biological samples
(21), and medical records (22) using identical DAG/certificate/braid
machinery. barraCuda's GPU math powers lattice QCD (07, 10b, 25),
protein structure (10a), bloom detection (04), and drug screening (12).
healthSpring's kinetic models (13) share ODE solvers with whole-cell
metabolism (ABG Paper E) and plasma transport (07).

Separating these into independent foundations would sever the
connections that make the ecosystem powerful. The foundation is one
interconnected graph. Expression documents zoom into subgraphs.

### The Continued Outcome

The constraints did not stop evolving after gen3. Eight springs absorbed
new science domains. The library-to-binary rewiring matured through four
tiers. The provenance trio became operationally validated end-to-end.
External contact — ABG community, Murillo group, Gonzales lab,
Kachkovskiy's spectral theory, Liao's anaerobic digestion research —
exposed systems the ecosystem had not yet expressed.

Every new contact, every new paper, every new spring experiment adds a
node to the lineage graph. The foundation grows as the ecosystem grows.

## 3. The 10 Domain Threads

### Thread 1: Whole-Cell Modeling

**The question**: Can NUCLEUS express an entire 14-year, 7-paper lineage
of whole-cell computational modeling — and fill the provenance gaps the
original authors couldn't?

**The lineage**: Karr et al. (2012, Cell) built the first *M. genitalium*
whole-cell model in MATLAB. Rees-Garbutt et al. (2020) used it for genome
minimization. Thornburg et al. (2022) built the first fully dynamical
JCVI-syn3A kinetic model. Stevens et al. (2023) simulated the entire cell
at molecular resolution. Thornburg et al. (2026) integrated everything
into a 4D cell cycle simulation.

**What NUCLEUS provides**: toadStool dispatches 28 submodels (Paper A) and
multi-scale coupled simulations (Papers E–G). NestGate content-addresses
every parameter (1,900+ from Paper A, BRENDA/TECRdb constants from
Paper E). The provenance trio tracks parameter inheritance across all 7
papers. petalTongue renders the 4D cell cycle as a live computation surface.

**Cross-thread connections**: Thread 7 (Anderson math for spatial
organization), Thread 3 (kinetic modeling shares ODE solvers with
healthSpring), Thread 4 (genomics pipelines for gene annotation).

**Expression document**: `expressions/ABG_WHOLE_CELL_REBUILD.md`
**Data sources**: `data/sources/thread01_wcm.toml`
**Validation targets**: `data/targets/thread01_wcm_targets.toml`

## 7. Foundation Evolution

The foundation is not a static archive. It grows through:

1. **New expressions** — as springs reproduce papers from new contacts,
   expression documents capture the per-thread details
2. **New data sources** — as spring experiments identify databases and
   accession numbers, data source TOMLs are populated
3. **New validation targets** — as NUCLEUS compositions produce numerical
   results, target TOMLs capture the expected values
4. **New cross-thread connections** — as products compose capabilities
   from multiple threads, the connection graph densifies
5. **New contacts** — as outreach reaches new faculty and communities,
   their work maps into existing or new threads

The foundation grows as the ecosystem grows. The soil deepens.
